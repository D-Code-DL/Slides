The program is held at Detroit Edison Public School Academy (DEPSA) Early College of Excellence, a charter high school just north of Eastern Market.

What Has Been Easy? Difficult? Fun?

Remembering the resource gap and checking our privilege--difficult.

(B) not everyone has a computer they can tinker on at home, so you need paper-friendly resources as well.

Forming relationships with the students--easy...

(K) Easy because we laid a good foundation by dedicating the first two sessions to getting to know each other and setting clear expectations.
This included:
-playing a game to introduce ourselves to one another (which the two of us used JavaScript to make)
-creating a team agreement all together, complete with norms we decided on together
-having a teacher they already trusted to be around at the beginning
(B) (adds more of her insight)

Keeping the lessons engaging, and trying to teach to multiple learning styles--difficult.

(K) The students gave us new ideas throughout the class, though, as well as through a survey we sent out at the halfway point of the program.

The challenge of keeping up with the students' growth and development --fun.

(B) It made us better teachers, as well as better students.

Scheduling--difficult.

(K) Things always change at schools, and people are busy: IB presentations, state testing that ends up needing to be redone. Also, we had our own crises come up at work at different times,sometimes needed to split our attention...or one or the other of us would be sick or traveling. It was important to be clear with students that this would be the case.

Watching the students light up when their code works--fun.

-(B) A personal highlight of Brandy's. Helping someone to "getting it" has been very fulfilling.

What Are Some Things We Didn't Think About?

(Ironically enough...)

(K) The kids who have smartphones use them for a lot more than we did or even do now. For many, it's the main way that they view the slides we send out and do research... the computer is almost just for typing code.
(B) Those involved in coding are involved in many other things, so attendance was not always 100%; had to be ready to send out whatever the lesson was, and to keep referencing those lessons (integrating review into lesson, instead of stopping to review).
(K) Students may have stressful home/personal lives that will negatively affect their participation in class and they need empathetic connections, not judgement.

Goals

What Lessons Did We Learn?

Re: Organization

  • Get the academic calendar with all academic/extracurricular days
  • A stronger system for keeping track of your own files
  • Creating more integrated lessons that incorporated more soft skills from the beginning.
(B) Academic calendar--so we could have planned for testing/mid-terms/finals and ceremonies/celebrations as well as vacations/days out.
File system--students switched computers a lot, or would forget flash drives
(K) Integrated lessons--coding skills are not enough, they need to know what being a coder is really like and what abilities are valuable in the industry

Re: Engagement

  • Create more out of class lessons to be delivered via email/text messaging
  • Use of more hands-on student-led activities
(K) Out of class lessons -- keep engagement high between sessions.
Hands-on activities--We'd want them to have to research to complete the tasks vs following our examples. The students should be driving their learning as often as possible.
(B) When students do their own research, they go far beyond what we would have covered ("5 minutes of struggle")

Re: Efficiency

  • Have students take an entry survey
  • Develop on codepen.io (or something similar) from the beginning
Survey: to create a baseline we could properly measure their development (in skill and interest) from
Codepen: so you can see how everything works together without the saving and loading, etc. Eventually we'd teach them that, but we could save the complication at the beginning
